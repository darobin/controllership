In modern web development it is common for third parties to be involved at all levels, from the CDN to distinct web and database hosting to libraries, APIs, services, and trackers for analytics, marketing, or advertising.

These third parties are not all equal, however. From a data protection perspective, some are merely service providers for the first party and whatever personal data they may have access to remains under the control of the party which which the user has a direct relationship; while others are third-party data controllers and have the right to independent secondary reuse of the data they obtain. This distinction is particularly important to rendering privacy-related decision but is unfortunately absent as a primitive of the platform.

This document explores ways in which this information could be captured and conveyed, use cases for making use of it, and mechanisms through which its presence and veracity could be enforced.

This document is just a quick and dirty proposal to support discussions, it does not necessarily represent the views of The New York Times at this point.
